Under cover of darkness, Paris and Helen fled Sparta. The prince of Troy had fallen deeply under Aphrodite's spell, and he convinced the Spartan queen to abandon everything she had known. Together they sailed across the wine-dark sea toward the walls of Ilium.

When paris arrived at the gates of Troy with Helen at his side, King Priam faced an impossible choice. Should he return the stolen queen and avert war? Or should he welcome his son and the woman promised to him by the gods? The old king, moved by his love for prince paris, decided to shelter them both within his city's walls.

The Greek kings, bound by their ancient oath to protect Helen's chosen husband, began to gather their armies. Agamemnon, lord of Mycenae, took command of the allied forces. Achilles, greatest of Greek warriors, agreed to join the expedition. Odysseus, known for his cunning, reluctantly left his island kingdom of Ithaca.

A thousand black ships set sail from the shores of Greece, carrying warriors determined to punish Troy for the actions of Paris. The ten-year siege was about to begin.
